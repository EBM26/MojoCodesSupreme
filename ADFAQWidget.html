What is the CADR for the AirDoctor?

CADR stands for Clean Air Delivery Rate. It’s the standardized system for reporting the effectiveness of a particular air purifier in removing contaminants from the air within a specific period of time. The CADR for the AirDoctor® is as follows: POLLEN: 377, DUST: 328, and SMOKE: 340.

How do you change the filters for the AirDoctor?

Changing the filters is easy and takes just minutes. Simply follow these step-by-step instructions (you’ll get a complete manual included in your shipment)

  • 1. Remove the filter cover (it snaps right off without any tools).
  • 2. Remove any plastic bag surrounding the new filter.
  • 3. Insert the filter into the purifier in the direction of the arrows on top of each filter. Always insert the UltraHEPA® Filter first, followed by the Carbon/Gas Trap/VOC Filter. Follow the directional arrows on the top of the filters.
  • 4. Turn off/reset your Change Filter alert by pressing and holding the Auto and Dim buttons until the alert light(s) flash 3 times and turn off.
  • 5. Turn the AirDoctor® back on and enjoy breathing the fresh, pure air.
