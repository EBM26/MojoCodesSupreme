HOW DO I KNOW IF I NEED TO PERFORM DESCALING MAINTENANCE?

Depending on the mineral content of your water, a new AquaTru should filter one tank of tap water in about 10 to 15 minutes. As soon as you notice that it takes twice as long to filter a tank of water, or about 30 minutes or more, you need to descale AquaTru. It is important that maintenance be done as soon as you notice a reduced flow rate. Failure to do so may result in the complete failure of AquaTru since operating AquaTru with very low flow rate may permanently damage the pump.

HOW DOES HARD WATER AFFECT MY AQUATRU?

If you have hard water, regular descaling maintenance of your AquaTru is required for it to operate properly and to have a long operating life. Mineral build up (called scale) in the AquaTru pump and the AquaTru Reverse Osmosis filter will result in reduced flow rates or in some cases a complete loss of flow. You may need to replace your RO filter more frequently.

DO I NEED TO REPLACE MY RO FILTER AFTER COMPLETING DESCALING MAINTENANCE?

Mineral build up (or scale) can accumulate in both the AquaTru pump and the AquaTru Reverse Osmosis filter, resulting in reduced flow rates (slower filtering times), or in some cases, a complete loss of flow. If this sounds like your AquaTru, you will likely need to replace your RO Filter in addition to completing the descaling process. If your filtering time remains at 30 minutes or more after completing the descaling process, you will know that you need to replace your RO Filter.
